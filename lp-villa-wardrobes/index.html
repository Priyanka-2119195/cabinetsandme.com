Walk-In vs Fitted

Which Wardrobe Is Right
for Your Villa?

The type of wardrobe that suits your villa depends on available space, the number of occupants using it, and what you want the room to feel like. Both are built to the same standard. Both are designed around your life.

WI
Walk-In Closet

A room designed
as a wardrobe.

For villas with a dedicated dressing room or master suite with adequate floor space. A walk-in becomes part of your home's architecture — island unit, full-height hanging, open shelving, mirror walls, integrated seating. Showcaseable in a way no fitted wardrobe can be. Ideal for 4BHK villas and above.

FW
Fitted Wardrobe

Precision-built
for the bedroom wall.

For bedrooms where space is optimised. Floor-to-ceiling, wall-to-wall fitted wardrobes eliminate dead space while delivering significantly more storage than any freestanding alternative. Swing door or sliding door. Every interior layout designed per bedroom occupant — not a standard module repeated three times.

IL
Integrated Lighting

Functional at 5am.
Beautiful at 8pm.

Sensor-activated rail lighting, drawer illumination and mirror backlighting — so the wardrobe is as functional before sunrise as it is when you are showing it to guests. LED systems are designed into the cabinetry at the manufacturing stage, not added as an afterthought.

CD
Custom Drawer Inserts

Designed for what
you actually own.

Jewellery trays with individual compartments, watch inserts, tie racks, folded-shirt modules, shoe display slots — every drawer designed around the specific items it holds. Not a generic pull-out in a standard box. The difference in daily use is immediate and permanent.
