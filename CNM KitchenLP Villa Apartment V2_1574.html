What Nobody Tells You Before You Sign a Vendor

You're Not Imagining It.
Getting a Kitchen Right Is Harder Than It Looks.

A rendering looks flawless until it meets your actual walls, your actual plumbing points and the way you actually cook. Most vendors don't discover the mismatch until installation day — same as you.

1
The Layout Problem

"The island looks great
in the brochure — not in my kitchen."

Standard layouts are drawn before anyone visits your specific home. Fixed points that should've been designed around end up jutting into your walking space, because nobody measured first.

2
The Material Problem

"My cabinet doors are swelling,
and it's only been two monsoons."

Cheap MDF cores with no proper edge-banding absorb humidity faster than anyone admits. By year two, doors stop closing flush.

3
The Accountability Problem

"Every vendor blames
the other vendor."

Carpenter blames the electrician, electrician blames the plumber, nobody owns the point that's now 4 inches off from where the hob needs to sit. You're the one chasing all three.

4
The Timeline Problem

"The 6-week kitchen
that took five months."

Manufacturing delays, site coordination gaps and no single point of ownership turn a straightforward project into an open-ended one. Nobody tells you until you're three months in.

We don't discover these problems on site. We design for them before the first cabinet is cut.

One Studio, Two Disciplines

Villa Kitchen or
Apartment Kitchen?
We Design Each, Differently.

Not every kitchen faces the same challenges. Villas and apartments come with different constraints and different opportunities — so before we design anything, we tailor our approach to which one is yours.

01
Open floor plates, no fixed reference points
Villa Kitchen
With no columns or builder-fixed points to anchor against, the risk is a kitchen that drifts — too big, too spread out, no working triangle. We design the layout around your cooking style and entertaining flow first, then let the space follow.
02
Fixed shear walls and structural columns
Apartment Kitchen
Structural columns intrude into kitchen footprints in nearly every apartment layout — our design team measures and works around them from the first site visit, never after installation begins.
03
Multiple independent contractors on site
Villa Kitchen
Civil, electrical, plumbing and carpentry are often four separate vendors with four separate drawings. We coordinate directly with your site team so the gas line, chimney duct and counter height are resolved before manufacture — not discovered after.
04
Tight installation windows in occupied towers
Apartment Kitchen
Society rules, lift bookings and shared corridors demand tighter coordination than a standalone villa. Our site teams plan installation around your building's access hours, not the other way around.
Materials & Hardware

The Humidity Problem
Nobody Warns You About.

Every kitchen sits in a different environment — humidity, ventilation, direct sunlight — and most carcass boards simply aren't built for it. We use FSC-certified, moisture-resistant boards and German-manufactured fronts with tolerances under 0.1mm, so doors still close flush after your fifth monsoon, not just your first.

Kitchen Questions

Before Your
Consultation

Genuinely differently. An apartment brief starts with the constraints — columns, beams, fixed plumbing points, society access hours — and we design the layout to work around them from the first site visit. A villa brief starts with your floor plate and lifestyle — cooking style, entertaining flow, sightlines — because without fixed reference points, an open kitchen can easily end up feeling disjointed. Same manufacturing standard, two different design conversations.
Builder-fitted kitchens are typically built to a fixed cost ceiling, not a fixed quality standard. Lower-grade boards, unbranded hardware and minimal edge-sealing are common ways that cost is controlled — which is exactly where wear shows up first. We never compromise on board grade or hardware brand to hit a lower price point.
No. Kitchen projects — villa or apartment — are sequenced so the rest of your home stays liveable. We schedule noisy or disruptive work around your routine, not the other way around.
For apartments, we coordinate directly with your facility manager before work begins — material movement slots, noise windows, and visitor passes are confirmed in advance, so installation isn't the day we find out the rules. Villas don't face this constraint, but we do coordinate with your other on-site vendors instead.
We design the layout around the column from the first measurement — sometimes that means a recessed unit, sometimes a feature ledge, sometimes a slightly reconfigured layout. It's a design decision made with you, not a problem boxed in after the fact.
Design, manufacturing and installation are all handled in-house by one team — there's no subcontractor chain for you to chase. One point of contact, start to finish, for either a villa or an apartment.
